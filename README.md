# Automotive-Investment-Case


This folder contains the complete project material for an automotive investment case developed for the Applied Corporate Finance and Private Capital course. The project analyzes three major automotive companies — Stellantis, Mercedes-Benz, and BYD — from a strategic, financial, market, stock performance, beta, and valuation perspective.

The purpose of the project is to compare different automotive business models within the global mobility industry: a mass-market multi-brand OEM, a premium automotive manufacturer, and a vertically integrated electric vehicle leader. The analysis combines qualitative industry research with quantitative financial modeling, share price analysis, beta estimation, and discounted cash flow valuation.

## Project Overview

The project focuses on the automotive sector and the ongoing transformation driven by electrification, software-defined vehicles, autonomous driving, Chinese OEM expansion, and changing capital allocation strategies. The investment case evaluates how Stellantis, Mercedes-Benz, and BYD are positioned within this environment and compares their financial performance, risk profile, market perception, and valuation potential.

The folder includes both the analytical workbooks used to build the financial model and the final presentation summarizing the investment case. Together, these files provide a complete view of the research process, from raw financial inputs and market data to final investment recommendations.

## Companies Analyzed

The project compares three companies with different strategic profiles:

- **Stellantis**  
  A global mass-market automotive group with a large multi-brand portfolio. The analysis treats Stellantis as a potential turnaround investment case, with particular attention to profitability decline, negative free cash flow, capital structure, liquidity, and recovery assumptions.

- **Mercedes-Benz**  
  A premium and luxury automotive manufacturer. The analysis focuses on its defensive positioning, strong brand equity, cash generation, premium margins, cost-saving initiatives, and exposure to China and EV transition risks.

- **BYD**  
  A vertically integrated electric vehicle and battery leader. The analysis evaluates BYD as a long-term growth case supported by scale, vertical integration, EV leadership, international expansion, and strong revenue growth, while also considering margin pressure, capex intensity, and valuation sensitivity.

## Methodology

The project combines several analytical approaches:

1. **Market and strategic analysis**  
   The project first examines the global automotive market, including EV growth, regional penetration, software-defined vehicles, autonomous driving, Chinese OEM expansion, and competitive positioning.

2. **Company analysis**  
   Each company is assessed based on its business model, geographic exposure, financial performance, EV strategy, competitive advantages, and main risks.

3. **Financial analysis**  
   Historical financial data is used to compare profitability, leverage, capital intensity, free cash flow generation, ROIC, WACC, and value creation.

4. **Share price analysis**  
   The project compares indexed stock performance, volatility, and return metrics to understand how the market has priced each company over the selected period.

5. **Beta estimation**  
   Stock return data is used to estimate beta against relevant benchmarks and to support the calculation of the cost of equity.

6. **Five-year business plan**  
   Revenue, EBITDA, working capital, capex, and free cash flow are projected over a five-year forecast period for each company.

7. **DCF valuation**  
   The projected unlevered free cash flows are discounted using company-specific WACC assumptions. Terminal value is calculated using a long-term growth approach, and the final valuation outputs are compared with current market prices.

8. **Investment recommendation**  
   The final section combines strategic positioning, financial performance, valuation output, upside potential, and risk factors to support an investment view for each company.

## Main Investment Case Logic

The final investment case presents three different investment profiles:

- **Stellantis** is analyzed as a speculative turnaround case, with large upside potential but significant execution risk.
- **Mercedes-Benz** is analyzed as a more defensive value case, supported by premium positioning, strong liquidity, free cash flow generation, and shareholder returns.
- **BYD** is analyzed as a long-term growth case, supported by EV leadership, vertical integration, and strong revenue growth, but with valuation sensitivity and high capital intensity.

## Academic Purpose

This project was developed for academic purposes as part of a corporate finance and private capital analysis. The files are intended to demonstrate the application of financial analysis, business planning, valuation techniques, and investment reasoning to real-world listed companies.

The project should not be interpreted as professional financial advice or as a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any financial security. All valuation outputs are highly dependent on the assumptions used in the models.
